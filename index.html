Reflection on Course Goals

In this course, my first learning goal was to develop a robust understanding of qualitative and quantitative research methods applicable to user-centered design. Considering we learned about over 20 methods and were able to employ eight with live participants, I believe this goal was well met. Secondly, I aimed to enhance my skills in creating and testing prototypes, ensuring they align with user needs and expectations. When creating the Lo-Fi prototype, we integrated all of our research into a simple paper interface. Although users didn't exactly jump out of their seats using the prototype, I learned a great deal about authentic user interactions. Lastly, I aspired to improve my ability to communicate research findings and design concepts clearly and persuasively to stakeholders, utilizing visual aids and presentations to make complex information accessible and actionable. Given that our course required multiple deliverables every week, ranging from testing plans to full research reports, I feel confident that this goal was achieved exceptionally well.

I thoroughly enjoyed my experience in the User Centered Research and Evaluation Course at Carnegie Mellon University. I am excited to apply what I have learned to my future work in the U.S. Army and beyond.
